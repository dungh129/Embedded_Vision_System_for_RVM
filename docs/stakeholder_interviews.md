# Stakeholder Interviews and Field Observations

## Overview

To complement the online survey, in-person interviews and field observations were conducted with users and an operator of the Olyns Cube Reverse Vending Machine.

The objective was to capture real-world experiences, operational challenges, and system limitations that may not be reflected in survey data.

---

## Customer Insights

### Customer 1

* Began recycling after the installation of the Olyns machine
* Collected recyclables during daily walks, in parks, and from coworkers
* Used the machine approximately **twice per week**
* Accumulated over **$600 in rewards over two years**

**Motivation:**

* Environmental concern
* Personal financial benefit

---

### Customer 2

* Recycled weekly for approximately one year
* Primarily recycled plastic bottles

**Motivation:**

* Convenient location near a shopping center

---

### Customer 3

* Experienced a **negative initial interaction** due to:

  * long wait times
  * full bins

* Stored recyclables for nearly a year before retrying

* Encountered system unavailability due to software updates

**Outcome:**

* Indicated willingness to abandon recycling if reliability does not improve

---

## Key Customer Insights

* RVM systems can **initiate and reinforce recycling habits**
* **Convenience and accessibility** are strong motivators
* **System reliability (availability, wait time)** directly affects user trust
* Poor user experience can lead to **complete disengagement from recycling**

---

## Operator Interview and Observations

### Operational Context

* One operator managed multiple units independently
* High-traffic machines required up to **6 collections per day**

---

### Observed Challenges

#### 1. Glass Handling Risks

* Glass bins were **heavy and unsafe to handle**
* Bottles frequently broke
* Collection bags were prone to tearing

---

#### 2. Sanitation Issues

* Residual liquids from recyclables caused:

  * odor
  * hygiene concerns

---

#### 3. Maintenance and Reliability

* Machine downtime (e.g., software updates) impacted usability
* Full bins reduced system availability and user satisfaction

---

## Key Operator Insights

* Operational safety must be considered in system design
* Waste handling (especially glass) introduces **mechanical and safety risks**
* Maintenance frequency and bin capacity are critical for continuous operation
* Sanitation and cleanliness directly impact user experience

---

## Engineering Impact

The stakeholder interviews informed several key system considerations:

* **Robustness**

  * System must operate reliably under real-world conditions

* **Throughput and Availability**

  * Minimize wait time and prevent full-bin conditions

* **Safety**

  * Improve handling of glass materials and reduce breakage risk

* **Sanitation**

  * Consider liquid containment and odor control mechanisms

* **User Experience**

  * Ensure consistent system availability and ease of use

These findings reinforce the importance of designing systems that balance
technical performance with real-world usability and operational constraints.

---

## Notes

* Observations are qualitative and based on a limited number of interviews
* Insights are used to guide design considerations rather than statistical conclusions
